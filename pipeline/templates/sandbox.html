How this works — and what it can't tell you

Your statement is rated by the same model, prompt, and rubric that rate the real statements on the front page, against the same officially agreed Weimar goals, and compared with the three capitals' average stances from the current edition's 14-day window. The text is sent to the judge and discarded — it is never stored, logged, or added to the dataset. (Only your email is stored, if you choose to leave one.) One honest caveat: a single LLM rating of a single sentence is noisier than a week of rated statements — treat the result as indicative.
